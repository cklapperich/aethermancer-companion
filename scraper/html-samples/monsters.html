The Monsters of Terastae defy the virtues of the world and are trapped in a cycle of death and rebirth. Aethermancers are individuals with the ability to bond with these monsters, helping them achieve Worthiness to escape this cycle.

In Aethermancer, the majority of monsters draw inspiration from various cultures, folklores, and other works of fiction, with some having significant alterations to suit the game's fantasy setting. It's important to note that these monsters may not necessarily be accurate representations of their respective origins.

Obtaining Monsters

When starting the game for the first time, the player chooses one of four Starter monsters—Cherufe, Jotunn, Minokawa, or Nixe. This monster becomes the player's first Soulbonded companion. Depending on the Starter, the player also receives a second Memento: Wolpertinger if Cherufe is chosen, Tatzelwurm if Jotunn or Nixe are chosen, or Orthrus if Minokawa is chosen. If the player is playing through the Tutorial, this second Memento is obtained during the first encounter that teaches how to craft a monster's Memento. Otherwise, it is given alongside the Starter's Memento.

A Memento is a permanent inventory item representing a monster the player has successfully acquired. It allows that monster to be summoned and Soulbonded. To obtain a monster's Memento, the player must use a Blank Memento on a Staggered monster during combat. A Blank Memento cannot be used if the monster is not Staggered or if its Memento has already been obtained—unless the player owns the "Soul Catcher" meta-upgrade. Any monster whose Memento the player holds can be Soulbonded by speaking to Lily in Pilgrim's Rest. Before each run, the player selects one Soulbonded monster to start with.

The Monster Shrine summons additional monsters into the player's party. It offers a random selection of monsters for which the player has Mementos but has not yet summoned, up to the number of Monster Souls held (maximum 15). Each area contains one Monster Shrine near its entrance, which becomes inactive after a single use. The player may have up to three monsters in the party at any time, with only one of each species allowed. If the party is full, a monster must be replaced to add another. A monster that dies in battle is removed from the party for the rest of the run, though it can still appear in Monster Shrines. To maintain a full team, the player is encouraged to rebirth another monster. The "Soul Catcher" meta-upgrade removes the restriction on using a Memento for monsters already acquired. It also allows the player to capture that monster and gain an additional Soul. Any monster captured this way is guaranteed to appear in the Monster Shrine of the next zone.

Each run begins with one Blank Memento and three Monster Souls, though meta-upgrades can increase these starting amounts. Additional Mementos and Souls can drop as loot or be purchased from the Merchant. At the end of a run, all active monsters are removed from the party. They return to level 1 and lose any Skills and bonuses gained during that run. However, Mementos remain permanent, and Worthiness earned by monsters carries over. Reaching Worthiness levels 2 and 3 grants new Perks, and the player receives Aether Crystals whenever a monster levels up.

Other Monsters

Seven upcoming monsters from the winners of the Monster Design Contest are planned to be added in the Alchemical Woods Biome update, estimated for release in Q2 2026. These monsters are Bronze Lion, Caduceus, Ettin, Living Armour, Philosopher's Stone, Ranger, and Stiltskin. In the same contest, Mothman and a fairy monster were also teased by the developers when those monsters, or similar concepts, were brought up during the event to avoid contestants creating similar submissions.

The Merchant is accompanied by a monster nicknamed "Sally," which appears alongside them, even in Pilgrim's Rest, though how this monster exists outside the Fractured Ruins is never explained. One of the Merchant's lines of dialogue there reveals that Sally is a Snallygaster.
